01 — Position

I sit between the technology and the road.

Australia's motorways now charge millions of vehicles a year without a single vehicle slowing down. That only works if the equipment above the lane, the software behind it and the people watching both are doing their job at 3am on a Sunday.

That's been my work for the last two and a half years at Kapsch TrafficCom — maintenance assurance for tolling infrastructure across the West Gate Tunnel in Melbourne, the Gateway and Logan Motorways in South East Queensland, and networks in New Zealand. Daily health checks, root cause analysis, technical direction to field crews, and a data layer I built to catch faults that never announce themselves.

Fourteen years sits behind that — intelligent transport systems, tolling, EV DC fast charging and HV/LV electrical construction, across India and Australia. And outside the day job, I build the AI/ML tools for the problems I meet at work.

02 — How a toll actually works

One vehicle. Three places it can go wrong.

The gantry is the first ten metres of a system that runs for weeks. My work lives across the whole chain — where the data is born, where it becomes money, and where you go looking for the faults that stay invisible.

  1. Roadside

    The data is born

    Passage detected, vehicle classified, tag or plate read. DSRC, imaging and classification readings fused into one statement about one vehicle at one instant — at 100 km/h.

    MLFF · ANPR · GNSS · DSRC · RSU

  2. Back office

    The data becomes money

    Matched to an account, assembled into a trip, priced, invoiced. Exceptions raised and resolved. Billions of records where "check the logs" stops being an instruction.

    Trip building · Transaction processing · Commercial back office

  3. Assurance

    You go find the fault

    Free-flow systems fail silently — a drifting RSU, degrading image quality, a mis-binning sensor all report as "up". Assurance is proactive by necessity, or the failure surfaces weeks later at the invoice.

    Condition monitoring · RCA · Anomaly detection
